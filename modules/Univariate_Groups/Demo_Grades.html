Background

A mathematics professor at Northland College has tracked the letter grade assigned to each student that completed a particular course for each of the last five academic years. The data can be found in MTHGrades.csv. The data are loaded below,1 and “W” (withdraw) and “I” (incomplete) grades were removed (use of filter()). The letter grades were ordered appropriately (using levels= in factor() in mutate()) and a second grade variable was created where “minus” and “plus” grades were converted to “whole”grades (use of mapvalues() in mutate()).2

#!# Set to your own working directory and have just your filename below.
grds <- read.csv("https://raw.githubusercontent.com/droglenc/NCData/master/MTHGrades.csv") %>%
  filter(!Grade %in% c("W","I")) %>%
  mutate(Grade2=plyr::mapvalues(Grade,from=c("A-","B+","B-","C+","C-","D+"),
                                to=c("A","B","B","C","C","D")),
         Grade2=factor(Grade2,levels=c("A","B","C","D","F")),
         Grade=factor(Grade,levels=c("A","A-","B+","B","B-","C+","C","C-","D+","D","F")))
